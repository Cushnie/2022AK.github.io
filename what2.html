Saturday, July 2

Picnic at Auke Bay

Location: Auke Rec Picnic Site

The Auke Recreation Picnic Area is on a beach on the northern shoreline of Auke Bay, and was once a village site for the Auk Tlingit Natives. Often in the summer, porpoises can be seen from the beach as they break the surface of the water. The area is a 14 mile drive from downtown Juneau north along Glacier Highway. The area is only one and a half miles north of the Alaska Marine Highway ferry terminal.

Getting There

Two buses are reserved for a 4:30pm pick-up to Auke Bay, with a 9:30pm return. We have the shelter all day and until midnight so people can go any time and stay later if they have alternate transportation. For the first bus, we have three pick-up locations.

  1. Downtown Douglas
  2. Breeze In, by the Juneau-Douglas Bridge
  3. Centennial Hall in the Center of town

The second bus is for anyone staying out in the Valley or by the glacier. We need to know if people are interested in a bus ride from the Valley to Auke Bay. And we need the addresses of where you’re staying. Each bus can carry up to 50 people.

If there is not enough interest, we’ll cancel one bus.

The cost will be approximately $20.00/per person round trip and will be paid to Dawn Walsh. A Lyft from Douglas to Auke Bay is approximately $46-60 one-way. There is free parking for those who drive instead of taking the bus.

Eating & Drinking

For food options, you can either bring your own cooler or picnic basket or purchase something from Forno Rosso Pizzaria. We did have another truck however they cancelled due to staffing issues. We are currently researching other options for a second truck. Please see the Facebook group page to vote for what kind of options you'd like (carnivore, vegetarian, gluten-free, etc.) as we want to ensure that we have enough for those with special dietary restrictions or preferences.

Sunday, July 3

Hike & Heritage Day

Location: Treadwell Mine

10:00am to 1:00pm Come explore and learn about the rich Walsh history in Ireland and Alaska at the Treadwell Historical Preservation & Restoration Society. For anyone interested, we’ll meet at 9:00am to run, hike or explore the trail. This event is for anyone who is interested in hearing family history, sharing photos, and viewing the family tree. This is an outdoor event so dress according to the weather.

The rest of the day is open for exploring, shopping, and relaxing. Don't forget about the fireworks after midnight!
