Juneau is Alaska’s closest “big” city to the Lower 48, which makes it surprisingly reachable any time of year. In just over a two-hour flight, Alaska Airlines can get you here on one of their daily flights from Seattle or Anchorage, multiple direct flights arrive daily. Charter, regional and major airlines all serve Juneau International Airport, Southeast Alaska’s air transport hub.

Cruising remains the most popular way to explore the Inside Passage; Juneau is a port of call for large and small lines, alike. For the independent traveler, the state-run Alaska Marine Highway ferry system serves more than 30 coastal communities. A true adventure, the 1500-mile Alaska-Canadian Highway—aka the “ALCAN”—crosses some of the world’s wildest country. But of course, any road trip to Juneau necessitates a leg at sea; the only “road” in is the Alaska Marine Highway.

Juneau is located in the middle of the Tongass National Rain Forest. We can receive anywhere from 60-90 inches of rain each year. Summer temperatures can range from 55°F-80°F and the weather can change quickly. We highly recommend that you bring clothes you can layer.

  • Pack a light shell for rain. This is highly recommended if you are going on the whale watching tour as it will help keep you dry and warm from the wind and salt spray.
  • A fleece, puffy jacket, or lightweight coat.The evenings can get chilly and if you are not used to cooler summers, you will be glad you did bring it.
  • A stocking cap or beanie.This is recommended for when it cools down, or to protect you from mosquitoes (the unofficial state bird of Alaska - the real state bird is the Willow Ptarmigan). We will have bug spray at our events.
  • Tennis shoes or walking shoes with a good tread for trails or boardwalks. The event on July 3rd is a 15 minute easy walk on a well maintained trail.
  • Shorts and a short sleeve or tank top. Yes, sometimes it gets hot and sunny.
Juneau is extremely casual and anything goes.
